The first advertisement for a microprocessor, the Intel 4004, appears in Electronic News. Developed for a Japanese caculator, it had the 2250 transistors and could make up to 90000 calculations/minute.

Dennis Ritchie and his team created the "C"programming language and released it to the public. It was based on the earlier language BCPL (Basic Combined Programming Language) and was a huge success.

The apple II was sold with main logic board, switching power supply, keyboard, case, manual, game paddles, and cassette tape containing the game Breakout, the Apple-II finds popularity in the common American household.

The Motorola 68000 microprocessor exhibited a processing speed far greater than other computers in the market. This high performance processor found its place in powerful work stations intended for graphics-intensive programs common in engineering.
